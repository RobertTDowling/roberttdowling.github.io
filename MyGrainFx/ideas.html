Robot Voice

  • Set the grain size small [picture goes here]
  • Set atomic algorithm [picture]
  • Play stretching time, pitch or scratch
The pitch of the Robot Voice changes with the size of grain.

Sing-Song Voice

  • Set the grain size small [picture goes here]
  • Set smooth algorithm [picture]
  • Scratch slowly or play stretching time [picture]

Both the Robot and Sing-Song Voice are a direct result of size of the grains. In the case of the atomic algorithm, all the grains are the exact same size, so when they are played back-to-back, the pitch corresponding to the grain size appears as a droning tone. In the case of the smooth algorithm, the grains are not all the exact same size, and the small variations in length create similar variations in pitch which

If the grain size corresponds to a pitch in the audible vocal range, 300-3000Hz, it can sound like singing.

Scratching Ideas

The scratch bar can be used in creative ways
  • Try to scratch forward at exactly the right speed to play normally
  • Try to scratch backward as the same speed
  • Put those together to get DJ FX
  • Hold your finger on the scratch bar to get a sustained effect
    • If the grain size is small, it will be more of whistle or hum
    • If the grain size is large, it will be more of a sample repetion effect
  • Hold you find on the scratch bar and roll it slightly left and right to get a vibrato effect between two pitches or samples
  • Type typing on the scratch bar

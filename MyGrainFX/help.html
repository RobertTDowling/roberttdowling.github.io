MyGrainFX Help

Overview

The Big Picture

MyGrainFX uses Granular Synthesis to make wonderful sounds from your own voice recordings. It uses different ways to chop up the recordings into little segments called sound grains. Grains are larger than the digital samples normally manipulated by computers. Each grain is like a little chunk of audio, long enough to have particular, unique sound, but short enough that adding or removing one here or there does not disturb the overall continuity of the recoding.

Tips for Better Recordings

  • Avoid having silence at start or end of recoding. Start talking right away and be ready to hit stop as soon as you can.
  • Make sure you talk as loud as you would on the phone: not so loud that you distort, and not so quiet you can't be heard.

Fun Things To Try

Robot Voice

  • Set the grain size small, around 3ms

  • Set atomic algorithm

  • Play stretching time, shifting pitch or
    scratching
The pitch of the Robot Voice changes with the size of grain.

Sing-Song Voice

  • Set the grain size small, around 3ms

  • Set smooth algorithm

  • Scratch slowly or
    play stretching time

Both the Robot and Sing-Song Voice are a direct result of grain size. Atomic grains are all the same size, so when they are played back-to-back, the pitch corresponding to the grain size sounds as a droning tone. Smooth grains are not all the same size, and the small variations in length create similar variations in pitch which "sings" when played.

CD Skip Glitch

  • Set the grain very large
  • Set the grain count high

  • Scratch or
    play stretching time
Playback will repeat segments over and over like a CD skipping.

Scratching Ideas


The scratch bar can be used in creative ways
  • Try to scratch forward at exactly the right speed to play normally
  • Try to scratch backward as the same speed
  • Put those together to get DJ FX
  • Hold your finger on the scratch bar to get a sustained effect
    • If the grain size is small, it will be more of whistle or hum
    • If the grain size is large, it will be more of CD skip effect
  • Hold you find on the scratch bar and roll it slightly left and right to get a vibrato effect between two pitches or samples
    • Roll slowly
    • Roll quickly
  • Type "typing" on the scratch bar

Melody Tip

The melody play works best with short samples less than 1/2 a second in length.

Texture Ideas

  • Set the grain around 20ms (50Hz)

  • Set the grain count high

  • Experiment with
    atomic and
    smooth grain boundaries
  • Play stretching time, or
    scratching
With a moderate grain size, very high grain repetition (big time stretches) give a slowly shifting texture used in a lot of ambient music.

Gender Changer

  • Set the grain around 20ms (50Hz)

  • Set smooth grain boundaries
  • Play shifting pitch
This effect does not work as well as some of the others, but with small pitch shifts, you can imagine your words in other voices.
